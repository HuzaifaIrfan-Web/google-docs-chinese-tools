Docs has no real ruby text, so annotations render as small superscript text right after each character, or as a grey line under the paragraph.

Google Docs can't detect keystrokes, so true live-as-you-type isn't possible. This re-applies tone coloring to the whole document about once a minute in the background. Annotations still need a manual Convert click.

Choose what to copy above. Copy uses your current tone-color/annotation style live — it works even if "Phonetic annotation" is off, and never changes the doc. Rich-text apps (email, chat, Word, other Docs/Slides/Sheets) keep the colors; plain-text targets get just the characters and/or annotation text.
